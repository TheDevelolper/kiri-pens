Hand Machining A Fountain Pen Quick Guide

2 April 2026

Build Process

Step 1 - Creating A Blank

1.1 Length of Material

You will need a rod of about 18mm outer diameter (The cap will be 17mm) should be a minimum of 187 mm (the longer the better).

PartDiameterLengthClearanceLength + Clearance
Barrel15 mm80 mm5 mm85 mm
Section12 mm30 mm5 mm35 mm
Cap17 mm62 mm5 mm67 mm
TotalsN/A172 mm15 mm187 mm

Notes: You can use multiple materials if you can’t make the minimum length. If you have more material then you can subtract 172 mm from your material length and divide by 3 to calculate your clearance.

1.2 Cut blanks to part sizes

Cut the blank into parts:

PartLength + 5mm Clearance
Barrel85 mm
Section35 mm
Cap67 mm

We’re now ready to work on the separate parts of the pen.

Step 2 - The Section

TODO: A better approach would be to keep the cap and the section as one piece initially, you could then make the section slightly larger. Then you’d be able to turn it down slightly.

  • Put Section Piece into chuck ensure it’s secure and square
  • Using an 18mm (or whatever is appropriate for your rod material) collet chuck, hold the blank for the nib section.
  • Use a barrel trimmer to square off both ends of the section and reduce the length to 31 mm

  • Use the small starter drill bit to create a small centre point to work from

  • Keep the starter bit inside the blank for the next part (gives the tenon more stength)

  • Use rectangular parting tool to create a tenon for the M10 thread, 8 mm length X ⌀9.7 mm, perfect size for the M10X1.0 die try to get this as smooth as possible (though don’t worry if you can’t it’s a nice to have).

  • Use the rectangular parting tool to bring the section body down to ⌀16mm leaving 4 mm to take off later

  • Get yourself a bowl of washing up liquid and water which will act as a lubricant for threading

  • Using an M10 die holder thread the end of the section slowly by hand using the washing up liquid as a lubricant

  • Drill a ⌀6 mm hole through the piece (frequently back out and clean, use beeswax on drill bit) and place on the mandrel to secure it

  • Use rectangular parting tool to get the diameter of the section body to ⌀12.8 mm

  • Pencil Mark a line 5 mm in from the nib unit side for the collar for the section

  • Using the slim parting tool make a tiny parting with a diameter of ⌀11.5 mm on the marked line

  • Pencil Mark another line 2 mm in from the nib unit side

  • Create a slope from the 2mm line to the 5mm line using the bevel as a guide

  • Create a slope from the threaded side to the smallest diameter point again using the bevel as a guide

  • Hold the section in a chuck and turn the increase the diameter of the 6 mm hole to 7 mm on the slowest RPM possible, use lubricant too, which should give you a smoother finish

  • Turn the section around in the chuck

  • Hold the nib section next to an ⌀8mm drill bit and mark from the very top of the nib housing (not the nib itself) to the top of the thread (should be about 12.9 mm), you can do the 1st 10mm by machine and the last 2 mm slowly by hand

  • Put the M7.5x0.5 tap into the tap holder and turn about 5 mm deep (by hand)

  • Now we need to drill out the collar, we can use an 9 mm drill bit and go in about 1-2 mm (slowly by hand), This should fit the collar of the nib unit snuggly into the section

  • Finish smoothing and polishing up the section
